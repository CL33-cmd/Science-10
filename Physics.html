Physics 10

Physics Unit Exam Notes

Distinguish between vectors and scalars

  • Vector: a quantity that has both direction and magnitude
    • an arrow above the variable indicates a vector quantity
  • Scalar: a quantity that has only magnitude or size

Perform unit conversions

  • dimensional analysis
    •
  • 1 hour = 3600 seconds
  • 1 day = 24 hours
  • 24 hours = 86,400 seconds
  • 1 kg = 1000g
  • 1 cm = 10 mm
  • 1 km = 1000 m

Calculate displacement

  • displacement = final position - initial position
  • up, right, north, east = positive
  • down, left, south, west = negative

Calculate average speed, distance, and time

  • average speed = distance / time
  • distance = speed * time
  • time = speed * distance

Determine slope of a position-time graph

  • slope = rise / run
  • the slope represents the velocity, as it is the change in position over time

Determine type and direction of motion of different graphs

  • Position-time graphs:
    • horizontal line indicates that the position of the object is not changing, and is therefore not moving
    • a curved shape indicated that the object is accelerating
  • Velocity-time graphs:
    • a horizontal line at zero indicates that the object has zero velocity and is therefore not moving
    • a line with constant slope indicates that the velocity of the object is changing at a constant rate, and is therefore accelerating
    • a curve down means the object is moving in a negative direction, accelerates in negative direction, and speeds up (acceleration is in the same direction as movement
  • a line down means that the object is accelerating in the negative direction (decelerating, slowing down)
  • a line up means the object is accelerating forwards (speeding up)
  • speeds up = acceleration is in same direction as movement

Calculate average acceleration, final velocity, initial velocity, and time

  • average acceleration = velocity / time = (final velocity - initial velocity) / time
    • velocity = m/s
    • time = seconds

Identify conditions for work to be done

  • mechanical energy must be transferred between objects
  • force must be applied
  • object must travel some distance
  • work must be done
    • if any of these are false, the answer = 0

Distinguish between different types of energy

  • Kinetic: the energy stored in an object due to movement
  • Gravitational: the energy stored in an object due to it's position relative to a specific reference axis
  • Elastic Potential: a stretched object has elastic potential energy if it can return to its original shape
  • Thermal: the total kinetic energy of the particles that make up a substance
  • Nuclear potential: energy stored in teh nucleus of atoms

Calculate kinetic and gravitational energy, mass, speed, height

  • kinetic energy = 1/2 (mass) (speed^2)
  • gravitational force = (mass) (acceleration due to gravity [9.81m/s/s)

identify fundamental SI units

  • work/energy(joule) = force(newton) * distance(meter)

Analyze force-distance graphs

  • area = work
  • calculate area of shapes to find the work done
  • if it is a curved shape, count the number of boxes, and find the area that way

Examples of conversions between forms of energy

  • fuel > driving = chemical > mechanical
  • using a pulley = mechanical > gravitational potential
  • picking up a ball then throwing it = kinetic > gravitational potential
  • shooting an elastic band = elastic potential > kinetic potential
  • powering heater with nuclear reactor = nuclear potential > mechanical > thermal
  • throwing a ball upwards = kinetic energy > gravitational potential

State First and Second Laws of Thermodynamics

  • First: energy cannot be created or destroyed, but can be transformed or transferred
  • Second: no energy conversion can be 100% efficient
    • some energy will always be lost to surrounding environment

Define and calculate efficiency

  • efficiency: a measurement of how much of the desired work or product is obtained from each unit of energy invested into that task
  • efficiency = output energy / input energy
